# Coach Playbook | AMA — Session Plan

**Format:** ~40 min | Audience: Insights + Perform PMs

---

## 1. Welcome + framing (2 min)

Set expectations upfront: this is a two-way session. The goal is that everyone leaves knowing where to find Coach context and who to talk to — not having memorised everything. The Playbook can guide you after the session.

---

## 2. Meet the Coach team (5 min)

Quick intros — who owns what. Worth anchoring on the post-reorg shape (Coach Foundations, May 2026) since many won't know the current structure. Reference the support table from the Playbook: PM (Opportunity Review), Design/UX (Quick UX Guidance), Engineering (implementation), Prompt Design (PSX), Legal.

---

## 3. Coach Playbook TL;DR (5 min)

Walk the six sections of the Playbook at pace — one sentence per section, not a deep dive:

- **0. Start Here** — what Coach is, how agents work, types of Coach experiences, self-serve vs. Coach Foundations responsibilities, the review process, what good looks like
- **1. Discovery & Opportunity Framing** — how to shape and prioritise a Coach opportunity, required SMEs
- **2. Experience Design** — principles, interaction frameworks, side panel guidelines, trust & explainability, content guidelines
- **3. Technical Implementation** — how to add Coach to your repo, evals, guardrails, security considerations, release readiness checklist
- **4. Validation & Readiness** — UXR checklist, practical guidance for testing Coach
- **5. Release & Launch** — rollout strategy, PMM, pricing & packaging, feedback loops

Close with: **what's coming** — The Playbook is a living document; sevearl sections still being built out - this is a beginning; help us understand what's missing; feedback welcome via #help_coach_playbook.

## 5. Ask Me Anything (~40 min)

Open with 3–4 pre-seeded questions surfaced via Slido ahead of the session, then open the floor for live questions from the same Slido board.

Suggested question to seed ahead of time (post in Insights/Perform channels ~1 week before):
> *"What's one thing you've always wanted to know about Coach or how we work?"*

Good fallback Qs if nothing comes in:
- What data can Coach actually see on a given page?
- How do admin settings work — who controls what?
- What does it take to add Coach to a new surface?
- How do flag rollouts work — who gates what?
