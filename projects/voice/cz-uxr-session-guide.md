## Opening

Before we start — just to set expectations: Voice is currently a desktop-only feature. How does that land for you? Is desktop where you'd naturally use Coach for coaching conversations?

## First impressions (icons)

Before we go to working prototype, we've a screen to show you. 

**Step 1: Look at icons (NO HOVER JUST YET)**

* Have a look at these two icons here
* What do you think they mean? What will happen when you tap each of them?

_**Look for:** See if the waveform icon naturally implies "dialogue" or if it's just seen as a "fancy mic."_ Do they think they both just mean 'record'?

###   
**Step 2: Label comparison**

_Show the two pairs (Noun vs. Mixed) side-by-side_

* If you wanted Coach to **speak to Coach and have Coach back to you** in real-time, which of these four labels makes that most obvious?
* Any words that feel unclear or confusing?

_**Look for:** Immediate reactions to 'Dictation' or 'Mode'._

## Dictation mode

* We're going to start with voice input, text output. (Don't say dictation - see which button they use) Go ahead and start a coaching conversation on a topic that's real and relevant to you right now. Use it for 3–5 minutes. _(Observe: do they find the controls, any hesitation, any drop-off)_
* How did that feel? Was the UI clear — did you know how to start, stop, and navigate without being told?
* How did the response speed feel? Did it ever break your flow or interrupt your thinking?  

## Conversation mode

* Now we'd like you to try voice in, voice out. (Don't say conversation - see if they know which icon to use…) Same idea: pick a topic and just go. _(Observe: reaction to the voice, any latency friction, drop-off points)_
* What was your immediate reaction to the voice itself — tone, pace, personality? Did it feel right for a coaching context?
* What was your reaction to the response time? Was it fast enough?
* Compared to dictation, which mode felt more natural? Can you see yourself using both, or defaulting to one?

## Voice preference

* We're trialling a few different voices. I'll play short samples of three voices.  
  Which feels most appropriate for professional coaching, and why?
* Question: Checking the sample dialogue is the same for each voice?

    I'm going to play three different audio samples for a specific AI voice. Each sample represents a different type of interaction: a greeting, a moment of empathy, and a moment of professional challenge. After you've heard all three, I'll ask about some questions.



### **Step 1: Play** 

_3 scenarios **OR** just one sample if that's all you have_

(Red = means scrap if you can't have different scenarios)

1. **Sample A (Greeting):** "Hi there. What's on your mind today?"
2. **Sample B (Empathy):** "That sounds like a demanding situation to be in. Let's take a moment to look at it together."
3. **Sample C (Challenge):** "You're focusing on how to get them to change. What would it look like if you focused on what's within your control instead?"

### **Step 2: Rate it**

Randomize so they're not always in the same order (i.e Baz, or whoever, isn't always first)

On a scale of 1 ('Not at all') and 5 ('Completely'), how would you rate this voice on the following?

1. Clarity: This voice is easy to understand
2. Trust: This voice sounds like someone you'd take serious career advice from
3. Approachability: This voice sounds like someone you'd feel comfortable talking to

###   
**Step 3: Emotional gut-check**

"In just **one word**, how does this voice make you feel?" (only works if the scripts for each voice is identical, otherwise the content will impact)

### **Step 4: Final call**

_Ask only after they have heard all the voice options._

**Pick one:** If you had to choose one of these voices to be your personal workplace coach for the next six months, which would it be? And why.

**Optional dealbreaker:** Was there anything about a voice you _didn't_ choose that felt 'robotic' or off-putting?

Red = means scrap if you can't have different scenarios

1. _**Clarity:** Tells us if **tech** is working. (e.g., "I can't understand what it's saying.")_
2. _**Trust:** Tells you if the **persona** is working. (e.g., "It's clear, but it sounds like a sales rep, not a business coach.)_
3. _**Approachability:** Tells you if the **relationship** is working. (e.g., "It sounds professional, but it feels insincere/cold")_  

## Utility & barriers

* What would need to be different for voice to become a regular part of how you use Coach?
