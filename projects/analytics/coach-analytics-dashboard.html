💬 Sample Convos
░ "My manager keeps avoiding the conversation…"
3 turns · resolved
▼
User: My manager keeps avoiding the difficult conversation about my performance. I've tried to bring it up twice but they change the subject.

Coach: That sounds frustrating — being left without clarity makes it hard to improve or feel secure. A few things worth exploring: Is your manager conflict-averse generally, or is this specific to you? And have you made the ask explicit, or left it open-ended?

User: Probably conflict-averse in general. I've hinted but not asked directly.

Coach: A direct, low-stakes request often breaks the pattern. Try: "I'd really value 20 minutes to get your honest read on where I'm at." Framing it as something you want — not something they owe you — makes it easier for them to say yes.

░ "We have a hiring freeze and my goals are stale…"
2 turns · resolved
▼
User: We have a hiring freeze and my goals are stale — they assumed two new hires that aren't coming.

Coach: Worth a reset conversation with your manager. You're not asking to reduce accountability — you're asking to redirect it toward what's actually achievable. Come with a revised version ready: "Given the freeze, here's what I think makes sense to focus on instead." Turns it from a complaint into a proposal.
